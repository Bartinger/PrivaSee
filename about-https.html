Who's this HTTPS and why does everyone want to connect with him?

TL;DR

HTTPS encrypts everything you do on the web, so a middleman (your ISP, etc) can't read it and laugh about the kitties you're looking at. This might not be so important for the average kitty-pic-collecting website, but becomes a serious problem if you're logging into a website (your password and / or account might get stolen!). As long as you're surfing at home, in a somewhat "secure" network, the risks of HTTP aren't too high. However, in a public network, for example one of a convention with 10.000 participants, there's a huge risk to lose control over your sensitive data!
